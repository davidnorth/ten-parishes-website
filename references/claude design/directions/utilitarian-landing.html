About the festival

An open invitation, from one studio to the next.

For ten days each July, the studios, sheds, kitchens and front rooms of ten Somerset parishes open to anyone who wants to walk in. Visitors meet the people behind the work, see how it's made, and — if they like — take something home.

The 2026 edition runs 1—10 July, organised by the Wiveliscombe Area Partnership. We're now collecting registrations from artists living or working in the ten parishes. Each artist gets a profile page, a printed trail‑map listing, and a spot on the festival route.

Who can register

Open to artists living or working in the ten parishes.

01

Artists & makers

Painters, sculptors, ceramicists, printmakers, jewellers, textile artists, woodworkers and other practising makers. Hobbyists and professionals welcome.

02

Group studios

Shared studios, collectives and pop‑up venues can register as a group. A single application covers up to eight artists at one address.

03

Venues & hosts

Have a barn, garden or village hall and want to host a visiting artist? Register your venue and we'll match you with someone nearby.

The ten parishes

Wiveliscombe and its neighbours.

Roughly thirty miles of country lanes around the Brendon Hills. Most studios are within a fifteen‑minute drive of Wiveliscombe.
